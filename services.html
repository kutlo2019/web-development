Services

STBK assists students with their student tier-4 visas. This visa needs to be applied for 6 months before the start of the course. We also help students obtain express visa, which can take up to 2 weeks.

STBK Offers The Following Services

VISA Applications

Students are assisted to get their tier 4 visa to start their study in the Uk. This includes assisting with payments to be made for the VISA.

UCAS Applications

The applications are done online through Universities and Colleges Admissions Services (UCAS) and STBK is an officially registered with UCAS as an application centre and students adviser.

Career Guidance

When it's time for considering career options, students can face difficult choices to make. Their strengths and weeknesses would be assessed so as they can be assisted in making informed decisions.

Expositions

We invite UK universities to come, meet and introduce themselves and their services to the students eligible for sponsorship. We are the official representatives of several UK universities.

Follow Ups

In instances where students encounter challenges with the universities set conditions we intervene on their behalf. ( eg EFL requirements, deposit payments & accommodation, etc.).

Pre Departure Briefing

We organize pre-departure briefing sessions, at the sessions we invite guests from different organisations to motivate & address the students on issues concerning various aspects of life in the UK.

UCAS PERSONAL STATEMENTS

To apply to most universities in the United Kingdom, you need to write in lenght the motivation for choosing the course or subject you would like to study. This is called a personal statement and is done through the UCAS site, it is used to support your application to study at a college or university.

What Do You Write About?

  • A personal statement tells colleges/universities about yourself!
  • You tell them why you are the most suitable candidate to study at their institution.
  • When applying for the same course/subject in multiple institutions we have to avoid writing the college or university name, this is because its advisable to write only one personal statement.
  • Some ideas to get you started:
    • Identify the skills and qualities required to do the course.
    • Talk about your ambitions and interests concerning the subject.
    • What makes you suitable for the course?
    • Are you in any social clubs, societies or sports teams?
  • If you want to visit the UCAS page on writing personal statements click here.
