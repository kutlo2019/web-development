Career Guidance

When it's time for considering career options, students can face difficult choices to make. We focus on their career desires, academic strengths and job market caps. STBK assists students in choosing courses the wish to pursue.

Choosing A Course

It is very important that students have to choose a course they will enjoy and will help them reach their career goals. We have noted important things students need to do to choose the right course for study:

  • Think about what you enjoy day to day
  • Explore many job sites and graduate career options to find which specific job field you'd enjoy working in
  • Note your career goals and qualification requirements

The Runway Organisation

The Runway is an organisation that is aimed at improving the transition phase that an international student faces when staying abroad, their objective is for the international student to return home well equipped and ready to contribute to the economy. They too offer excellent services such as their "wa mmata" student mentorship programme.

Wa Mmata Career Mentoring

Having a big brother or sister allows us to learn valuable lessons from one who has gone before us. They offer advice, guidance, and reassurance about various things. Because they have been there before you, they may also be able to assist with answers to "How can I progress my career in this industry line?"

Our Career Mentoring will be a six-month programme in which you receive a minimum of six contact hours with your mentor for support with professional and, in some ways, personal development, and awareness. Lowerclassmen match up with Upperclassmen following similar career paths. The match-up also caters to similar interests as much as possible.

Mentors need not live in the same country/city as their mentees. However, they will be studying or have completed the same or similar course. Close, personalised interactions will create space for specialised advice and guidance to students. The program creates an environment of greater security for students to disseminate accurate, up-to-date, and relevant information. The programme offers a grand opportunity to build networks, find trusted confidants, and form lifelong friendships, which prove to be valuable to any student.

The program is open to new/incoming students and current students that seek to have a student mentor. New/incoming students are encouraged to sign up for the buddy scheme when they have confirmed their course and institution of study.

Benefits

  • Learn more about a specific career or industry
  • Increased confidence and motivation
  • Enhanced inter/intrapersonal skills
  • Improved communication skills
  • Improved critical thinking and problem-solving skills
  • Explore your ideas, values, and future career possibilities
  • Gain insight from someone who has faced similar challenges or concerns in the world of work
  • Develop professional skills and contacts

How It Works

  • Individuals interested in becoming either a mentee or a mentor, or both, may register here.
  • Pairing considers the course and interests of both parties
  • Mentors will initiate communication
  • Regular meetings and communication will be as agreed by the pair, with at least six hours of contact time
  • The Runway will conduct regular check-ins to assess programme success
  • Mentees may request a change of mentor if necessary
